Tips:
  • Check to see if a news article is an opinion piece or not. The URL or header of the page will often have the word "opinion" if it's an opinion piece.
  • Opinion pieces will typically take one side or the other. Their language and the arguments they present should give away which side of the issue they support.
  • News articles that are not opinion pieces will often just give the facts and should be classified as neither. However, sometimes authors have their implicit biases. Look out for those!
  • Be sure to read through the whole page to understand the author's viewpoint. For example, an article might start with arguments against marijuana legalization, but later show why those arguments are wrong. That would make it pro-legalization.
  • A website that does not really discuss the issue of legalization should be classified as neither.
