Write customer reviews for iPod. Do not copy a review you find online. We will not pay for copied text.

Guidelines:

  • Provide the exact product name and model (if applicable).
  • Do NOT copy text from some other source. Do NOT plagiarize.
  • Write an honest and useful customer review.
  • Share personal stories about your experience with the product.
  • List good and bad aspects of the product.
  • Make comparisons between this product and similar products.
  • Talk about the product's value given its price.
  • Try to mention the product name/model in the review.
  • Use correct spelling and grammar.
  • Avoid excessive punctuation and capitalization.
  • The review should be long enough to provide useful information, but not too long that it causes the reader to lose interest.

Your product reviews can be positive or negative. Just be honest and write a real review
Use these GOOD example reviews as a guideline:

Positive review Negative review
"A great everyday camera" 4 out of 5 stars
I purchased this camera after my Nikon Coolpix S3000 lost its shutter button because I dropped it on a marble floor. Got the Canon Powershot SD1300IS and have to say, I love it! I took pictures of my dog running through wildflowers in the wooded area near my home. They look awesome! The pictures were clear, crisp and vivid -- especially compared to the Nikon. What I like about the Canon is that it is versatile to the owner. I mostly use the Program setting because it allows me to set the ISO and flash balances, but I sometimes use the auto options provided. The only downside is the shutter speed is not as fast as digital SLR cameras I've used. At just over $100, the camera provides wonderful value. 		"A camera with too many limitations" 1 out of 5 stars
Similar to the old Canon model, the Canon Powershot SD1300IS takes good, clean photos, and it's compact. However, instead of having an external menu for choosing modes (such as low-light, macro, or action shot), you need to toggle through multiple submenus on the display. I couldn't find a way to change the setting in less than 1 min. By the time I was ready to take a picture of a moving target, the moment had passed. When the camera petered out after two weeks, I was glad to have an excuse to purchase something better. I would rather spend a little more money to get the features I want. I ended up buying the Panasonic Lumix DMC-FH20, which has much better menu system for customizing photos.
